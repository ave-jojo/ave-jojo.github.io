What is Jojo's Universe?

It seems I should explain what I mean by saying Jojo's Universe.

This is a galaxy in the constellation Andromeda which is located about 10 million light years from the Milky Way. You, guys from Earth, call it NGC 404, not very beautiful but rationally.

It is located within 7 arc-minutes of second magnitude star Mirach, making it a difficult target to observe or photograph and granting it the nickname "Mirach's Ghost". Due to its proximity to Mirach, finding this tiny galaxy is quite simple. Direct your telescope towards Mirach and - ta-dam! - You're at the place. Yeah, this dim speck of light right near the star - I'm there.

But there is a problem to see NGC 404. A galaxy with a brightness of 11.2 more than 4300 times dimmer than a star. As a result, even the slightest haze or optical pollution by dust scatters starlight across the field of view and destroys the ghostly image of the galaxy. So we need a strategy.

Popular wisdom says: to find a complex object, so close to the overwhelmingly bright interference, you need to separate them.

Choose an eyepiece that provides a high enough magnification to do this, and take the Mirach out of sight, hunting the galaxy. To detect a galaxy, a pair of a 4-inch f/9.8 refractor and a Pleuml 12-mm eyepiece can solve this problem. Despite the fact that the field covers more than 30 minutes across, it is narrow enough so that you can move the star away and distinguish the galaxy.

If visibility allows, try a similar set, but with the addition of Barlow's high-quality twofold lens. This additional touch should make the observation of the galaxy a little easier, but only with a sharp focus.

Use to check the focus on the star, and then remove it from sight and wait a few seconds to eye adapted again to the dark.
